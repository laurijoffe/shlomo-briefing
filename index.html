The Orchestration Gap

Think of orchestration like a conductor in a symphony. One musician (agent) playing perfectly doesn't make music—you need a conductor coordinating timing, dynamics, and when each instrument enters. In AI, that conductor is the orchestration layer. It manages context passing between agents, ensures one agent's output becomes another's input, handles failures gracefully, and keeps the whole system coherent. Without it, you get agents working in isolation producing disconnected outputs. With it, you get emergent intelligence that's greater than the sum of parts.
