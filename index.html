ⓘ Teaching Moment

Orchestration vs. Automation

Think of automation like a assembly line worker who follows one exact job—if they hit a problem, they stop. Orchestration is like a team supervisor who watches multiple workers, knows what each specializes in, assigns work intelligently, and reroutes tasks when bottlenecks appear. In AI, automation executes rules (if X then Y). Orchestration coordinates multiple agents, decides who does what, handles failures, and manages shared memory across a workflow. Most enterprise AI fails because they have great models but no orchestration layer.
