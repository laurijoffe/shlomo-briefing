ⓘ Teaching Moment

Context Compaction in Multi-Agent Systems

Imagine you're running a relay race where each runner (agent) must remember everything the previous runner learned. Without stopping to summarize, the baton (context) becomes heavier and slower with each handoff. Context compaction is like a coach summarizing key strategies between legs of the race—each runner gets the essential knowledge without carrying 26,000 tokens of raw conversation history. This keeps latency low (0.71s instead of 9.87s) and lets agents reason clearly.
