ⓘ Teaching Moment

Agent Orchestration as Coordination Layer

Think of agent orchestration like conducting an orchestra. A single musician (agent) can play beautifully, but an orchestra needs a conductor to ensure violins don't drown out cellos, everyone stays in tempo, and the final piece sounds coherent instead of chaotic. In multi-agent AI, the 'conductor' is the orchestration layer—it decides which agent speaks when, ensures their outputs connect properly, and maintains quality control so one agent's mistake doesn't cascade through the entire system. Without it, specialized agents create beautiful individual work that doesn't integrate into reliable business processes.
