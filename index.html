Teaching Moment
ⓘ Teaching Moment

The governance paradox: Autonomy vs. Control

Imagine you hire someone brilliant but unpredictable to run critical operations. The more powerful you make them, the more damage they can do if they go rogue. Early agents were like interns—supervised, limited scope. Now they're executing complex workflows across enterprise systems in milliseconds. Traditional security (build first, audit later) doesn't work. You need "runtime security"—the ability to enforce policy at the exact moment an agent acts, like having a supervisor watching every keystroke in real-time. That's the shift April 29 signals.
