ⓘ Teaching Moment

The Harness Layer Problem

Think of an AI agent like a chef. The model is how good the chef's intuition is, but the harness is the kitchen—where they find tools, manage workflow, and coordinate with other chefs. For years, we had brilliant chefs but broken kitchens (proprietary, black-box integrations). Claw Code releasing its open harness shows the field finally recognizing: the real engineering value isn't the model, it's the orchestration layer that lets agents actually *work* reliably in production. Same lesson applies to Shlomo: your frontier models matter, but the hub that routes them, stores context, enforces values, and coordinates spokes is where the advantage lives.
