Teaching Moment
ⓘ Teaching Moment

Hub-and-spoke vs. monolith

Think of a family office (Shlomo's user) like a wealthy person managing multiple business interests. Instead of hiring one super-genius advisor who tries to understand *everything*, you hire a **hub** (a family office manager) who coordinates with **spokes** (specialists—tax lawyer, investment banker, real estate expert). The specialist doesn't need to be smarter than the generalist; they just need deep expertise in one thing. As your wealth grows complex, the hub's job isn't deciding on every investment—it's orchestrating which specialist handles which decision, making sure they share what they learn. Modern AI is moving here: instead of one giant model, you have specialized agents (each knows one domain really well) coordinated by an orchestration layer (the hub) that decides who talks to whom and when.
