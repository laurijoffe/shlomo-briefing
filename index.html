Teaching Moment
ⓘ Teaching Moment

Orchestration vs. Coordination

Think of orchestration like a conductor leading a symphony: each musician (agent) plays their part, but the conductor controls timing, transitions, and ensures all sections work together. Coordination is just musicians agreeing to sync up. In 2026, enterprises realize coordination alone fails—they need a conductor (orchestration layer) to manage cost, prevent redundancy, and enforce governance across multiple agents working simultaneously. Without it, your multi-agent system becomes an expensive mess of overlapping work.
