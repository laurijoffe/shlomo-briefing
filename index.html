Teaching Moment
ⓘ Teaching Moment

Agent Staleness & Decay

Like fruit ripening in a bowl, an agent's memories have expiration dates. A user preference from 2024 stays useful until it doesn't—then becomes dangerously wrong. You can't just store memories and retrieve them. You need decay functions that age out low-relevance data (like how your attention naturally fades from old gossip) and detection systems for when high-relevance memories go stale (like realizing a friend moved cities six months ago). Mem0's dynamic forgetting applies literal decay scores; most systems haven't figured this out yet.
